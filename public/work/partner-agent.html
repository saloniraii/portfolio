Why isn't the portal working?

In collaboration with our Research team, we conducted a deep research into this problem. The core of the report can be boiled down into these three points.

Insight 01

The portal assumed captive attention

81% of the customer base (Scale-up + Micro-SaaS) had no dedicated channel managers to drive adoption. For them, the portal was a sunk cost.

Insight 02

Partners already had a workspace

WhatsApp, Slack, SMS. That's where deals moved and questions got asked. The portal was a side quest.

Insight 03

The jobs were conversational

Check pricing. Register a deal. Ask about MDF. These aren't GUI workflows — they're questions. The exact shape of a conversation.

Securing the Bet

Making design work read as a revenue opportunity

The vision wouldn't matter if it stayed in Figma. We worked on the strategy proposal, framing the investment through three lenses.

Market positioning

No competitor offered headless, conversation-native partner management. This would make Salesforce first.

De-risked execution

A phased roadmap where each release delivered standalone value — foundation, then skills, then channels.

Revenue model

A usage-based Flex-Credits pricing structure that made the agent accessible across partner tiers without cannibalizing existing licenses.

The proposal secured an 8-person team, VP sponsorship, two Principal Architect reviews and three pilot commitments.
